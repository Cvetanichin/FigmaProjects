🏛️ Human Rights Protection Project

North Macedonia Gender Equality & Human Rights Initiative

🎯 IMPACT

To protect the well-being, human rights and fundamental freedoms, with focus on girls, women and vulnerable groups in North Macedonia

⬇

🎲 OUTCOMES

Outcome 1: Building alliances and partnerships with the media with a focus on the promotion of human rights and the prevention and protection from disinformation, manipulation and threats.
Outcome 2: Strengthening the judicial system of North Macedonia to protect women, vulnerable groups and human rights defenders from discrimination, hate speech, violence and attacks, with a focus on gender equality.
Outcome 3: To reduce inequalities by promoting and providing access to social services led by local CSOs and public institutions that respond to the specific needs of vulnerable people, including LGBTI, women and girls.
⬇

📊 OUTPUTS

Output 1: The skills of media and journalists are increased to investigate and report on gender-related issues, appropriately respond to hate speech and gender stereotypes and provide access to accurate and fact-based information.
Output 2: The Constitutional Court can prosecute all instances of violence, to safeguard human dignity, prevent future human rights abuses, and implement relevant international standards and policies that protect social justice effectively.
Output 3: Non-discriminatory and affordable community-based services for vulnerable and marginalised groups are provided in three local municipalities, led by qualified practitioners and human rights defenders.
Intermediate Output: The General public, together with the right holders and duty bearers, can recognise gender based violence and adequately respond to the threats and better understand issues related to sexual education and gender diversity.
⬇

⚡ MAIN ACTIVITIES

WP 1: Transformative role of media
  • • Mentorship program for journalists on gender stereotypes, gender equality and exposing fake news
  • • Production of investigative journalist stories on gender related movements
  • • Training for journalists on ethical and gender sensitive reporting
WP 2: Countering legislative setback
  • • International study visit on preventing backlash in constitutional rights
  • • Workshop on international human rights legislation for Constitutional Court
  • • Development of educational materials for practitioners
WP 3: Access to Services for marginalized communities
  • • Training of healthcare and social service providers
  • • Training for civil society organizations
  • • Sub-granting scheme for local social services
WP 4: Communication and awareness rising
  • • Publishing journalistic articles and project content
  • • Social media campaign exposing fake narratives
  • • Conference on equality and safeguarding gender rights
