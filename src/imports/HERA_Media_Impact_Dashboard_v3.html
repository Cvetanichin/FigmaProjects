💡 Key Strategic Insights

✅ Strong Female Engagement

68.8% weighted female audience share on Facebook — significantly above sector benchmarks. GBV and femicide content achieves 79–84% female engagement. This validates content strategy alignment with target groups.

⚠️ Digital Growth Needed

Social media delivers only 24.7% of total reach. To close the 109.8K gap by June 2027, digital channels must grow by ~154%. Monthly requirement: 9,150 additional reach/month.

📺 Single-Outlet Risk

Sitel TV accounts for 45.1% of total reach (130.8K). Over-reliance on one partner creates vulnerability. Alsat (12.2%) and Telma (9.9%) need active strengthening through formal agreements.

🔍 Investigative Content Multiplier

Investigative pieces average 12K reach per story vs. 2–7K for standard news. The "Next Wave" anti-gender series achieved 13.8K. Recommendation: Double investigative output to 4–6 pieces annually.

🌍 LGBTIQ+ Coverage Gap

Only 1 story directly addressing LGBTIQ+ rights. The HIV discrimination piece (3.6K reach) demonstrates audience receptivity. A dedicated LGBTIQ+ content series targeting Radar.mk and YouTube is needed.

📊 Male Audience Strategy Required

Anti-gender movement investigations reach 39% male audience — the highest cross-gender engagement. Toxic masculinity and equal parenting content can serve as entry points for male audience expansion.

📋 Audience Gaps & Strategic Flags

🚩 Low male reach in GBV content

GBV/femicide stories average 20% male engagement. Reframing narratives around male bystander responsibility could improve male engagement without alienating women.

✅ Anti-gender investigations reach men (39%)

Highest cross-gender reach. Documentary format drives male curiosity. Scale this for broader male audience engagement strategy.

⬥ Unknown audience 1.3% — data quality flag

4 stories show zero or minimal tracking. Activate Facebook Pixel on all content URLs and ensure outlet-level analytics sharing agreements.

✅ Strong alignment with project beneficiaries

68.8% female reach means primary beneficiaries are engaging. Retain this focus while expanding strategies to include secondary audiences (male allies, policymakers).
